# Brand Pattern: BMW

## Lessons & Core Philosophy
BMW represents **industrial precision and angularity**. The UI alternates between incredibly dark, cinematic automotive photography and pure white content blocks. The defining trait is **zero border-radius** (sharp corners) everywhere, combined with a stark typographic contrast: whispered, light-weight (300) display headers against extremely heavy (900) navigation elements.

## Trade-offs
- **Pros:** Exudes power, precision, and luxury engineering. Highly structured.
- **Cons:** Very rigid and cold. Sharp corners can feel aggressive in a friendly, conversational consumer product.

## What NOT to Copy
- **Do not mix sharp corners with rounded corners.** BMW commits 100% to zero border-radius. If you adopt sharp geometry, apply it systematically.
- **Do not copy their ultra-tight line-heights** (1.15) for long-form reading. It works for BMW because their text blocks are short and marketing-focused.
